## Executive Summary

The 2024–2025 startup failure wave represents a definitive inflection point: the collapse of the "growth-at-all-costs" era and the emergence of a new standard demanding sustainable unit economics, genuine AI differentiation, and robust financial governance. Twenty notable failures reveal eight recurring failure archetypes that, when inverted, define the most defensible venture opportunities available today.

## Cross-Framework Failure Analysis

### Medici Lens: Relationship & Network Failures
The majority of failures lacked **ecosystem ownership** — they built point solutions rather than platforms. Convoy had no network effects. Jasper had no community moat. Builder.ai had no trust infrastructure. The Medici principle of *wealth through enabling others* was absent: none of these companies made their ecosystem richer.

### Rothschild Lens: Information & Infrastructure Failures
Most failures exhibited **information asymmetry blind spots** — they did not own the data layer. Adept AI had no proprietary training data. Sumo Logic ceded the data layer to AWS. The Rothschild principle of *owning invisible infrastructure* was violated: they built on top of infrastructure they did not control.

### Buffett Lens: Moat & Economics Failures
Every failure violated at least one Buffett principle: **no durable moat, no margin of safety, no cash flow discipline**. Getir had negative unit economics at scale. Vroom had no operational moat. Dave had no switching costs. The Buffett principle of *rejecting vanity metrics* was ignored — all prioritized GMV, DAU, and growth rate over contribution margin and retention.

## The 8 Universal Failure Archetypes

| Archetype | Pattern | AI-Native Inversion Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| **Fake AI Trap** | Human labor disguised as AI; no genuine automation | Verifiable AI-native operations with transparent automation metrics |
| **Thin Wrapper Syndrome** | No proprietary data; easily replicated by foundational models | Deep vertical AI with domain-specific data flywheels |
| **Hardware Scaling Chasm** | R&D success ≠ manufacturing success | Asset-light hardware intelligence (software that optimizes hardware) |
| **Unsustainable Unit Economics** | Growth funded losses; no path to contribution margin | Outcome-based pricing with AI-driven cost structure optimization |
| **Regulatory Blind Spot** | Operating in legal gray areas; targeting vulnerable users | Compliance-first architecture; regulatory moat as competitive advantage |
| **Commoditization Collapse** | Adjacent to hyperscalers or foundational models | Niche vertical depth where hyperscalers won't go |
| **Market Timing Failure** | Built on temporary demand; no durable behavioral shift | Identify permanent behavioral changes accelerated by AI adoption |
| **Governance Failure** | Poor financial controls; lack of investor transparency | AI-native financial governance as a product feature |
